# Code of Conduct - Weapon of Math Destruction

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone participating in this behavioral profiling project, regardless of:

- **Experience Level**: From beginners to expert researchers
- **Technology Stack**: Unity, Node.js, PostgreSQL, web technologies
- **Background**: Academic, industry, hobbyist, or activist
- **Identity**: Age, race, ethnicity, gender identity, sexual orientation
- **Ability**: Physical, mental, neurodiversity, or accessibility needs
- **Beliefs**: Religious, political, or philosophical perspectives
- **Geography**: Country of origin, language, or cultural background

## Our Standards

### ü§ù Positive Behavior

#### Inclusive Communication
- **Respectful Language**: Use inclusive terminology and avoid stereotypes
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Consider global audience and diverse backgrounds
- **Accessible Communication**: Provide alternatives for different needs and preferences
- **Multilingual Respect**: Welcome non-native English speakers and be patient

#### Constructive Collaboration
- **Helpful Attitude**: Support learning and share knowledge generously
- **Mentorship**: Guide newcomers and provide growth opportunities
- **Quality Feedback**: Offer constructive, specific, and actionable suggestions
- **Recognition**: Acknowledge contributions and celebrate achievements

#### Ethical Considerations
- **Privacy Advocacy**: Prioritize user rights and data protection
- **Transparency**: Open discussion of limitations and potential harms
- **Research Integrity**: Maintain scientific rigor and honesty about findings
- **Social Responsibility**: Consider broader impact of behavioral technologies

### üö´ Unacceptable Behavior

#### Harassment and Discrimination
- **Personal Attacks**: Insults, name-calling, or belittling language
- **Discriminatory Content**: Slurs, stereotypes, or exclusionary language
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Threats**: Intimidation, harassment, or harmful suggestions
- **Unwanted Contact**: Persistent messaging after requests to stop

#### Unethical Practices
- **Data Exploitation**: Suggesting malicious use of behavioral data
- **Manipulation**: Promoting harmful influence techniques
- **Privacy Violation**: Encouraging breaches of user consent
- **Academic Misconduct**: Plagiarism, data falsification, or misrepresentation
- **Commercial Exploitation**: Using research for harmful commercial purposes

#### Disruptive Behavior
- **Spam**: Excessive posting, off-topic content, or self-promotion
- **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or bad faith arguments
- **Vandalism**: Damaging project resources or documentation
- **Sabotage**: Intentionally breaking systems or misleading users
- **Legal Violations**: Encouraging illegal activities or harmful content

## üõ° Reporting and Enforcement

### Incident Reporting Process

#### How to Report
1. **Direct Contact**: Email [conduct@project.org] with detailed description
2. **GitHub Issues**: Use `conduct` label for public tracking
3. **Private Messages**: Contact maintainers or project administrators
4. **Anonymous Reports**: Anonymous reporting form available on project website

#### Report Content
- **What Happened**: Clear description of the incident or behavior
- **When and Where**: Timestamps and location (platform, thread, etc.)
- **Who Was Involved**: Specific individuals or groups (if comfortable sharing)
- **Impact**: How it affected you or your participation in the project
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, logs, links, or other supporting material
- **Resolution Request**: What outcome you would consider fair and appropriate

#### Enforcement Actions
- **Warning**: Private notice explaining policy violation and expected behavior change
- **Temporary Suspension**: Removal from project spaces for specified period
- **Permanent Ban**: Removal from project for severe or repeated violations
- **Content Removal**: Deletion of problematic content or contributions
- **Public Statement**: Notification to community about action taken (when appropriate)

### Response Timeline
- **Initial Response**: Within 48 hours of report receipt
- **Investigation**: 3-5 business days depending on complexity
- **Resolution**: Within 10 business days of report receipt
- **Follow-up**: Check with reporter to ensure satisfactory resolution

## üîÑ Consequence Process

### Violation Categories

#### Minor Violations
- **First Offense**: Warning and education about policy
- **Minor Impact**: Request to edit or remove problematic content
- **Good Faith Assumption**: Assume misunderstanding rather than malicious intent
- **Restorative Focus**: Education and community repair over punishment

#### Moderate Violations
- **Repeated Behavior**: Temporary suspension (3-7 days)
- **Community Harm**: Public apology and corrective action requirement
- **Privilege Suspension**: Temporary loss of commit or merge rights
- **Mentorship Requirement**: Assigned guide for reintegration

#### Severe Violations
- **Malicious Intent**: Immediate and permanent ban from project
- **Legal Concerns**: Report to appropriate legal authorities if necessary
- **Safety Threats**: Immediate action to protect community members
- **Research Misconduct**: Notification to academic institutions or employers

### Appeal Process
- **Timeline**: 7 days to appeal enforcement decision
- **Reviewer**: Different from original enforcement decision-maker
- **Evidence**: Opportunity to provide additional context or evidence
- **Final Decision**: Appeal decision is final and binding

- **Report Concerns**: Help maintain safe environment by reporting violations

- **Cultural Awareness**: Consider own biases and communication style impact

- **Empathy Practice**: Consider impact of words and actions on others
- **Accountability**: Take responsibility for mistakes and commit to improvement
